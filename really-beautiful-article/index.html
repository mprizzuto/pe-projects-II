Info for home bakers

The list of reasons to bake

(and break) bread at home are endless. Now more than even in a post-pandemic world, where people are going out less and food prices are rising. The benefits of being a home-baker include

  • control over ingredients. When you buy bread from a store, it is usually heavily processed. The healthiest bread has only four ingredients
    • flour
    • water
    • salt
    • yeast
  • cost effective. a good loaf of artisan bread can cost $8+ dollars. Why not spend that money on a bag of flour and make multiple loaves of bread instead?
  • it's therapeutic! Baking bread at home is good for your mental health. There is nothing like watching the flour absorb the water and mixing all the ingredients together to distract you from your problems.

Bakers math

I know, math is scary, right?! not really, atleast when you have the foundations down. When reading bread recipes, you may encounter something called the baker's percent

When calculating how much of an ingredient to add to a batch of dough, us Bakers weight the flour and base the percent of the ingredients on the total amount of flour. Say you have 500 grams(g) of flour.

  • water
    • if we want the dough to be 80% hydration, 80% * 500g = 400g of water
  • salt
    • Recipes vary, though it is standard for bread dough to usually have around 2-2.5% of salt(non-iodized is best). so, 2.5 * 500g = 12.5g salt
  • yeast(sourdough starter or instant yeast)
    • again, this is up to you. some recipes call for more or less of a rising agent. Less yeast means more time to develop flavor, but longer rising times.10% starter or .5% to 2% yeast. lets choose sourdough starter for our recipe. 10% of 500g = 50g of active(recently risen) starter

Wheat flours

a wheat flour is defined as a flour originating from the milling of wheat grains.

All-Purpose-Flour
A great multi-purpose flour. A pantry staple
Pastry flour
A low-gluten flour that is perfect for a tender and flaky crust
Cake flour
Similiar to pastry flour, with a slightly lower gluten content.
Bread flour
a high-protein flour perfect for all breads
00 flour
a high-protein finely milled flour
Self-rising flour
perfect for biscuits, pancakes, and muffins, this is a mixture of all-purpose flour, baking powder, and salt
Sprouted flour
the seed of the plant was allowed to germinate before it was dried and milled. This process is rumored to be more nutritious
Durum flour
Mostly associated with semolina flour, durum is an excellent choice for pasta and breads.

gluten free options

While the term flour is most synonymous with the milling of gluten-based grains like wheat, barley, or rye, not all flours have gluten. Here are some gluten free flour options

Rice flour
made from white rice, usually white but sometimes brown.
Corn flour
Made from ground corn. Often used in cornbread and tortillas.
Almond flour
Usually made from finerly-ground blanched almonds, this flour lends a nutty flavor to the final product.
Coconut flour
Made from ground coconut meat, coconut flour has a higher fiber content which helps promote healthy digestion
Chickpea flour
Made from ground chickpeas, chickpea flour is common in Indian and Middle Eastern cuisine.
Buckwheat flour
Made from ground buckwheat and despite the name, is a seed and not a wheat. Commonly used in pancakes and bread.
